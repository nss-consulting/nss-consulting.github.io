Some Guiding Questions to Develop Your Research Plan

How to develop your roadmap

Sometimes I receive requests from prospective clients that center on a particular methodology or analytic strategy. While these types of requests are very focused on a particular methodology, it often comes to light once we talk about the problems they want to solve and what they want to do with the data that the method theyâ€™ve requested is not the method they need. This is why, before we start to talk about methods, I want to understand your business problem or why you are interested in doing the research. Specifically, I want to understand who the research is for (e.g., your product team, your marketing team, your communication strategies team, etc.), what you hope to learn, and what you plan to do with what you learn. Once I have this information, I can help build out a comprehensive plan with recommendations for the methodologies and analytical techniques that best meet your needs.
