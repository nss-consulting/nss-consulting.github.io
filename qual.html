When to Consider Qualitative Methodologies

  • To gain a deeper understanding of individuals behaviors, attitudes, and needs
  • For topics where you want to be able to ask follow-up questions or probe for additional information
  • To understand how decisions are made/li>
  • To uncover unmet needs and barriers to particular behaviors/li>
  • In cases where it may be important to understand respondent tone or body language
  • For topics where it might be important to first establish rapport with participants
  • To observe how individuals interact with products or concepts

Primary Data Collection for Needs Assessment

End Client: Public School District
My Client: Consulting Firm
My Role: Lead Analyst

The consulting firm was tasked with conducting an inclusive needs assessment to inform the Districtâ€™s program and service planning. The district wanted to understand the current behavioral health needs of students in the school district and the extent that those needs were being met by existing structures of support. The primary aim of the work was to understand how students, parents, and faculty were experiencing the available mental health services in the district and identify areas where they might be able to step in to further meet their goals of accessible and equitable care.

After co-creating a project plan with the end-client, I collaborated with the consulting project team to develop a series of interview and discussion guides to gather perspectives from students, parents/guardians, mental health providers, school staff, faculty, and administrators (separately). I conducted 45-minute interviews with administrators and moderated separate 90-minute focus groups with school staff/faculty, mental health providers, and parents/guardians in the district. The purpose of these discussions was to illuminate gaps in support, identify strengths in current programs and services, and provide these stakeholders with a platform to share solutions and priorities to improve student behavioral health outcomes.

I was responsible for developing the analytic strategy and leading the analysis of the data. I developed the initial draft of the codebook and worked with another analyst to refine as we as we began to code interviews and focus group transcripts in Nvivo. I conducted thematic analyses for the parent focus groups, administrator interviews, and the faculty/staff focus groups.

I developed a topline report for the consulting team that identified key findings and recommendations for the end client. These were incorporated into the presentation slides and final report that were delivered to the school district.
