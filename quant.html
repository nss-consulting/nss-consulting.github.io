Survey Design and Implementation: Helping the client learn about their customers prioritize product feature releases, and develop a solid communication strategy.

Survey Design and Implementation

End Client: State Extension Agency Licensing Software

My Client: Consulting Firm

My Role: Research Consultant

The consulting firm was tasked with developing a communication strategy for the release of major software updates to a current product along with the launch of a net new online virtual community. The end client wanted to better understand current and former users of their software and shape the development of their future online virtual community.

I worked with the client to understand their key objectives and proposed a comprehensive research plan that outlined initial and longer-term research strategies to meet their goals. The first step of the plan was a baseline survey of current and former software users to identify each of the following

  • What is the profile of the current and former user?
  • How are/did individuals use the older version of the software?
  • What is their overall customer experience?
  • What are user experiences with current product features
  • What do users want from the software and communication about the product in the future?

I then developed an outline of the baseline survey with an analysis plan, describing how each of these questions would be addressed in the results of my 15-minute online survey.Once the survey was approved by the end client, I programmed the questionnaire into an online survey tool. I monitored data collection, cleaned the data file for analysis, and analyzed results -- using the analytic plan developed prior to launch to ensure I was making sure that the end client had the data that they needed to make key business decisions.

In the reporting, I identified key insights and provided initial recommendations based on the findings. As part of my aim in equipping them with the tools that they needed to continue to gather feedback from their customers on a more regular cadence, I provided a shortened version of the baseline questionnaire that they could incorporate into their regular processes. I also created a "how-to" analysis guide so that they could feel confident interpreting results.
