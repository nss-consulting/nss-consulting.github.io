Survey Design and Implementation

End Client: State Extension Agency

My Client: Consulting Firm

My Role: Research Consultant

My client, a consulting firm, was tasked by a state extension agency with developing a communication strategy for the release of major software updates to a current product, along with the launch of a new online virtual community. Along with these aims, the agency wanted my client to help it better understand the current and former users of its software and to shape the development of a future online virtual community.

I worked with the agency to understand their key objectives, and I proposed a comprehensive research plan that outlined initial and longer-term research strategies to meet their goals. The first step of this plan was a baseline survey of current and former software users to identify each of the following:

  • What is the software user profile?
  • How do/did individuals use the older version of the software?
  • What has been their overall customer experience?
  • What are user experiences with current product features
  • What do users want from the software and communication about the product in the future?

I then developed an outline of the baseline survey, alongside an analysis plan. This plan articulated for my client how each of these questions would be addressed by the 15-minute online survey. Once the agency approved the survey, I programmed the questionnaire into an online survey tool. In the reporting, I identified key insights and offered initial recommendations based on the findings. As part of my aim in equipping them with the tools that they needed to continue gathering feedback from their customers on a more regular cadence, I also provided them with a shortened version of the baseline questionnaire that they could incorporate into their regular processes. I also created a "how-to" analysis guide so that they could interpret their future results with insight and confidence.
